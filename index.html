About

Research
Overview

I am a Research Scientist II at CIRES, embedded within NOAA's Physical Sciences Laboratory at the University of Colorado Boulder. My work lies at the intersection of applied mathematics, dynamical systems, fluid dynamics, and data-driven modeling, with a focus on improving forecasting and state estimation for chaotic systems such as the Earth's atmosphere and oceans.

I have developed and implemented mathematical methods for subgrid-scale ocean parameterization, objective scale separation in multiscale flows, reduced-order stochastic modeling, and data-driven augmentation of numerical ocean models.

More recently my research has advanced along two complementary directions: AI–physics hybrid frameworks that improve forecasts from imperfect physics-based Earth system models, and pure AI emulators of the coupled Earth system jointly modeling ocean, atmosphere, land, and sea ice.

Looking ahead, I aim to strengthen the reliability of exascale AI emulators — understanding what they learn, how design choices affect stability and generalization, and how to estimate uncertainty reliably using principled stochasticity. I am also interested in AI–physics hybrid methods using differentiable dynamical cores (e.g., JAX-GCM, DYNASORE) that are interpretable and deployable in real forecasting workflows.

Focus Areas

Research
Interests

🌊
AI Earth System Emulation

Coupled AI emulators for ocean, atmosphere, land, and sea ice to accelerate medium- to extended-range forecasting.

🔀
AI–Physics Hybrid Frameworks

Cross-attractor transforms and related methods for correcting biased or incomplete Earth system model outputs.

📊
Stochastic Parameterization

Physics-based stochastic subgrid-scale schemes for ocean density, energy backscatter, and ensemble S2S statistics.

📉
Reduced-Order Modelling

Data-driven low-dimensional representations of multiscale ocean variability for analysis and prediction.

🎲
Uncertainty Quantification

Principled stochastic extensions to AI emulators for reliable uncertainty estimation and extreme event prediction.

🔬
Data Assimilation

Assimilating observations into ML weather prediction models, including surface pressure into operational systems.
